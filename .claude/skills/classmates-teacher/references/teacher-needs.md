# What Teachers Actually Need

Based on research with Scottish primary school teachers.

## Daily Needs
- See at a glance who played today and who didn't
- Check if assigned games were completed
- Identify pupils who are struggling (below 1 star consistently)
- Quick add/remove pupils as class changes

## Weekly Needs
- Review class progress trends
- Identify which games/topics are hardest for the class
- Create new assignments based on gaps
- Check mastery progression

## Termly Needs
- Generate progress reports for parents' evenings
- Export data for headteacher/SEN coordinator
- Map progress to CfE outcomes for professional judgement
- Archive end-of-year data

## Pain Points (from competitor research)
- Sumdog: Too complex to onboard, too many settings
- Purple Mash: Expensive, overwhelming feature set
- Glow: Teachers only use it for email
- General: Too many logins, too many platforms

## What Classmates Should Be
- Zero friction: teacher signs up, class plays in 2 minutes
- One screen tells the whole story
- Actions are obvious, not buried in menus
- Reports generate in one click
- Works on the school tablet the teacher carries around

## Current Gaps
- No search/filter on pupil roster
- No per-pupil drill-down with trends
- No PDF/CSV export
- No CfE mapping
- Assignments only cover 10 of 38 games
- No class analytics (which games are hard/easy)
- No editable teacher profile (school name, display name)
- No bulk CSV import for roster
